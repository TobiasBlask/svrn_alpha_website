The impulse from the October 2025 low remains, in our read, the cleanest count on any large-cap US equity. Five waves up, a text-book extension in wave 3, and now a pullback that has the proportions — and, crucially, the volume signature — of a fourth-wave correction rather than the opening leg of a bear trend. We say "crucially" because it is the volume, not the count, that resolves the ambiguity.

On the April 12 break below 870, we saw the first real test of the constructive interpretation. The stock did what the alternate count needed it not to do: it made a lower low on falling volume. That is the fingerprint of a fourth. It is not the fingerprint of the first leg of an ABC.

What the zone is made of

The 820–860 band is not chosen for rhetorical neatness. It sits at the 0.382 retrace of the full wave 1–3 impulse, coincides with the prior break-out shelf from March 3, and overlaps with the largest unfilled volume node on the 2024 profile. Three independent methods — wave structure, price structure, volume structure — point at the same twenty-dollar band. That is what we want before we size.

What would change our mind

A daily close below 780 opens the door to the alternate count — an ABC correction that would test the 710 pivot. We would not be short there; the structural thesis remains intact down to 640. But we would step aside from new longs, and we would communicate that decision on the day it happens, before the close. That is the deal.

The bigger question — the one we keep being asked — is whether the cycle itself has peaked. Our answer is: not yet, not on this structure. Semis are a cycle, yes. But cycles end with blow-off volume and exhaustion breadth, not with an orderly fourth-wave sitting inside its 0.382 retrace. When the cycle ends, this page will say so.
